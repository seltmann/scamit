Orange County Environmental Specialist

Orange County Sanitation District

Principal Environmental Specialist

7 June 2011
The Orange County Sanitation District (OCSD) seeks a Principal Environmental Specialist for its Infaunal Taxonomy Program. Located in southern California, OCSD is a public agency that is dedicated to maintaining world-class leadership in wastewater and water resource management, operating and maintaining the third largest wastewater treatment facilities east of the Mississippi River.
Under general direction the Principal Environmental Specialist serves as a project leader with a wide variety of technical and administrative responsibilities within the District's Environmental and Ocean Monitoring Laboratory. This position will have primary responsibility for management of the Infaunal Taxonomy Program. The successful candidate will possess the equivalent of a Bachelor's degree from an accredited college or university with major course work in biological, physical, or environmental science, including coursework and lab work in invertebrate zoology. A minimum of five years of increasingly responsible experience in an environmental laboratory or field collections environment, with three years at a level comparable to a Senior Environmental Specialist with the District is required. Other requirements include: a minimum of two years of recent experience identifying marine invertebrates and familiarity with the taxonomy of at least two taxa groups (e.g. polychaetes, crustaceans, mollusks, echinoderms, or members of the “minor phyla”); knowledge of wastewater treatment and associated processes, invertebrate zoology and the principles of taxonomy; and experience with compound and dissection microscopes and micro-dissection techniques.
